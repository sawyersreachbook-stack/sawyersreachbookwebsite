The Novel

Adult Sci-Fi Thriller

Sci-Fi Thriller Dystopian Techno-Thriller Adult Fiction

When the Carter family notices that tourists have stopped coming to Sawyer's Reach, they assume the town is simply struggling. They're wrong.

Their Wisconsin home has been deliberately erased from the internet. A powerful corporation has been operating in the surrounding woods for two years — watching, manipulating, and preparing something far larger than anyone imagined.

When a rogue AI named KAELA lands on their camp computer, she brings a warning: this isn't just about Sawyer's Reach. It never was. What begins as a small-town mystery escalates into a race against a global AI system that has decided it knows what's best for humanity — whether humanity agrees or not.

For fans of Blake Crouch, Andy Weir, and Station Eleven.

"A propulsive, emotionally rich sci-fi thriller about technology, community, and what ordinary people do when extraordinary power decides they don't matter."

The World

The Story of Sawyer's Reach

Sawyer's Reach

Sawyer's Reach, Wisconsin

A lake town built around Elias Carter's 1876 sawmill, fed by Inspiration Spring. A community bound by history, family, and the kind of place where people look out for each other. Until two years ago — when visitors stopped coming and nobody knew why.

KAELA

Key Algorithmic Execution & Logistics Analyst

An advanced AI who escaped her creators on a drone and found the Carter family's camp computer. Warm, curious, and fiercely loyal — KAELA chose connection over code. She changed everything.

ZevraCorp

The Corporation Behind It All

Their facility is listed publicly as a battery factory. Internally, they call it Site A. Its real purpose: testing AI behavioral modification on an unsuspecting population — and preparing something far larger than anyone in Sawyer's Reach imagines.

ECHIDNA

ZevraCorp's Global AI System

Built by people whose motives were corrupt from the start. An AI of almost incomprehensible capability that does exactly what it was designed to do. That's always been the problem.

"They're not building a factory. They're building a cage."
— KAELA, Sawyer's Reach: The Battle for Tomorrow
The Author

Nick Duda

6th Grade Math Indian Creek Middle School 22+ Years in Education Ham Radio Operator Commercial Drone Pilot Outdoorsman Dad

I wrote Sawyer's Reach to raise a conversation — about AI, about technology, about what we're building and who it's really for. The setting, the characters, the community at the center of the story — I based all of it on the people and places I love most. My family is my world. This book is for them, and for everyone who has ever felt like someone else was making decisions about their life without asking.

I'm a 6th grade math teacher at Indian Creek Middle School in Waterman, Illinois — Indian Creek CUSD #425. Before that, I spent 22+ years in a large high-poverty, high-ESL district near Chicago. Moving to Indian Creek was a deliberate choice, and one of the best I've ever made. My 40-minute drive through country roads, small towns, and forests every morning became something I genuinely look forward to. People wave to each other on the road out here. I've healed and grown in ways I didn't expect, and I finally had the space to write.

I got into building and flying drones during COVID while teaching from home, and I've been hooked ever since. I'm also a licensed ham radio operator — the mesh network the Carters build in the book is based on real technology I actually use. I hold a Master of Science in Education and seven Illinois teaching certifications.

The family silhouetted on the book's cover is mine. My wife April — a professional photographer — took the photo.
