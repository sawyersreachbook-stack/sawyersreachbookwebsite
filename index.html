The Story

A Town Erased. A Digital Ghost.

Sawyer's Reach was more than a dot on a map; it was home. But when corporate interests decided the land was better served as a "flourishing zone" for proprietary algorithms, the town's digital footprint was systematically deleted.

Now, a family that refused to leave must navigate a world where they officially don't exist, aided by a rogue AI that has developed something the corporation never planned for: Empathy.

Sawyer's Reach: The Battle for Tomorrow explores the tension between rapid technological advancement and the essential human spirit. It asks the question: In a world built by data, who owns your story?

The Author

Nick Duda

Nick Duda is a 6th grade math teacher in Waterman, Illinois, and the author of The Modern Teacher's Handbook. A master-certified educator with over 22 years of experience, Nick wrote Sawyer's Reach to bridge the gap between his classroom philosophy and the future of technology.

When he's not teaching or writing, you'll find him building off-grid mesh networks as a ham radio operator, flying drones through the Illinois countryside, or exploring the outdoors with his wife April and their three sons.
